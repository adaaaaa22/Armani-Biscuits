# Armani-Biscuits
The  Armani Biscuits Sales Performance Dashboard is an interactive business intelligence project developed to analyze sales performance, product profitability, customer purchasing behavior, and regional distribution across biscuit product categories.
# 🍪 Armani Biscuits Sales Performance Dashboard

## 📌 Project Overview

This dashboard was designed to help stakeholders quickly identify:

- High-performing products
- Profitability trends
- Sales performance by region
- Preferred payment methods
- Monthly sales movement
- Revenue and profit metrics

The goal of this project is to transform raw sales data into **actionable business insights** that support strategic decision-making.

---

## 🎯 Business Problem

Businesses often struggle to answer important performance questions such as:

- Which products generate high sales but low profit?
- Which regions contribute the most revenue?
- What payment method do customers prefer?
- How do sales fluctuate monthly?
- Which products should be prioritized?

Without a centralized dashboard, identifying these trends can be time-consuming and inefficient.

This project addresses that challenge by building a **single interactive dashboard** for performance monitoring and insight generation.

# 📌 Dashboard Walkthrough

## 1️⃣ Performance by Region

This visual shows sales distribution across different cities/regions.

### Key Findings:
- **San Antonio** recorded the highest sales performance (**7.34M**)
- **San Jose** and **Philadelphia** closely followed
- **Chicago** generated the lowest regional sales

### Business Insight:
Regional performance varies significantly, suggesting opportunities for:

- Regional marketing optimization
- Sales strategy adjustments
- Inventory redistribution

---

## 2️⃣ Products with High Sales but Low Profit

This chart compares **sales performance against profit generation**.

### Key Findings:
Some products generate strong revenue but produce relatively low profit margins.

Examples include:
- Oatmeal Cookies
- Cinnamon Swirls
- Butter Crunch

### Business Insight:
High sales do not always translate into profitability.

Potential reasons:
- High production cost
- Pricing inefficiencies
- Discounting strategies

### Recommendation:
Review pricing and cost structures for high-volume but low-profit products.

---

## 3️⃣ Proportion of Payment Method

This visual analyzes customer payment behavior.

### Key Findings:
- **Cinnamon Swirls** contributes the highest payment proportion (**4.1M**)
- Product purchase value decreases gradually across categories.

### Business Insight:
Understanding customer purchase patterns helps optimize:
- Promotions
- Product bundling
- Revenue forecasting

---

## 4️⃣ Distribution of Sales by Biscuit Brand

This visual highlights the sales contribution of biscuit brands.

### Key Findings:
- **Shortbread** generated the highest sales (**14M**)
- **Vanilla** followed closely (**12M**)
- **Chocolate Delight** showed the lowest sales contribution

### Business Insight:
Top-performing brands should receive:
- More promotional budget
- Better stock availability
- Product expansion opportunities

Low-performing products may require:
- Rebranding
- Discount campaigns
- Product repositioning

---

## 5️⃣ Trend of Sales by Month

This line chart analyzes monthly sales performance.

### Key Findings:
- Sales remained relatively stable from **January to July**
- A major decline occurred around **August**
- Sales stabilized at a lower level towards year-end

### Business Insight:
The drop may indicate:
- Seasonal buying behavior
- Reduced demand
- Operational disruptions

### Recommendation:
Conduct further analysis to identify causes behind the sales decline.

---

## 6️⃣ Prioritized Products

This visual ranks products based on performance.

### Key Findings:
Top-performing products include:

1. **Cinnamon Swirls**
2. **Butter Crunch**
3. **Lemon Crisps**

### Business Insight:
These products should be prioritized for:
- Marketing campaigns
- Inventory allocation
- Upselling strategies

# 🧮 DAX Measures Used

Some of the DAX measures created include:

```DAX
Total Sales =
SUM(Sales[Sales])

Total Quantity =
SUM(Sales[Quantity])

Total Profit =
SUM(Sales[Profit])

Average Revenue Per Product =
AVERAGE(Sales[Revenue])

Profit Margin % =
DIVIDE([Total Profit], [Total Sales], 0)
```
